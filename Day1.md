# Day 1 - My Programming Journey

## What is Programming?

Programming means giving step-by-step instructions to a computer.

A computer follows **EXACT instructions**.

### Example 1: Tea Making Logic

1. Take water and milk in equal portion
2. Add tea powder and boil it
3. Add sugar
4. Mix

### Example 2: Bad Instructions

Suppose we give these instructions:

1. Boil water
2. Add tea powder
3. Pour tea into a cup
4. Add sugar

The computer will still follow the instructions exactly.

Result:
- Sugar may not dissolve properly.
- The tea may not taste good.

The computer does not know the best method.

It only follows the instructions provided.

This is why programmers must give clear and correct instructions.

---

## Another Example

Instruction:

1. Put rice in a cooker
2. Switch on the cooker

What is missing?

- No water was added.

The computer (or machine) will still execute the instructions.

Result:
- Rice will not cook properly.
- The output will be incorrect.

The machine cannot guess that water is needed.

It only follows the given instructions.

## What I Learned Today

- Programming means giving instructions to a computer.
- Computers follow instructions exactly.
- Computers do not think like humans.
- Missing or wrong instructions produce wrong results.
- Python uses `print()` to display output.

### Programming Rule #1

**Garbage In, Garbage Out (GIGO)**

If we give bad instructions, the computer will still execute them and produce bad results.
